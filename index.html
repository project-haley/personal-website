My Vision
Since childhood, accesible software has played an important role in my life. Whether it was online flash games, educational software, or a cellphone, this technology has been at my fingertips for much, much longer than it hasn't. I think most people my age could say the same.

We live in a physical world inseparable from a virtual one. We owe it to ourselves to make use of that entanglement and create something really incredible. We owe it to each other to make that something accessible.

That's where web-based applications come in, and that's exactly what I've set out to do.
About Me
I am a self-taught programmer with a focus in web development using the ASP.NET MVC design pattern. I'm currently based in Huntsville, AL, where I work full-time and practice software development in my free time. I earned my B.S. in Chemistry in 2017 and attended an Atmospheric Science graduate program in 2018 with the intent to enter academia as an instructor. I quickly learned that this path was not what I wanted. After withdrawing from this career path in 2019, I took with me a love of programming and decided to pursue web development in order to create useful, accessible applications.

My first programming language was Python, which I used primarily for scripting data analysis in the laboratory. Soon came HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and eventually C#. I discovered ASP.NET as an entry point into professional software development and committed to learning a design pattern that would help me get my feet wet.

Now with my first deployed application, I have begun developing further applications (more to come!). I have also begun searching for junior-level developer positions and am looking for employment.
